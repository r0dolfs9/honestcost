Brand Imagery · 02

Cars, but
without the showroom.

HonestCost talks about cars the way smart buyers talk about cars — clearly, without the gloss. The imagery follows: honest light, daily contexts, no overproduced studio shots. When in doubt, choose the photo that looks like a real morning.

01 · Principles

Three rules.

Everything else flows from these.

Rule 01

Daylight, not showroom.

Cars photographed in real life — overcast morning, parking garage, a quiet street. Avoid spotlit studio rotations and glossy renders. The product is honesty; the imagery should look honest too.

Rule 02

People over heroics.

Where humans appear, they're doing something normal — reading the calculator on a kitchen counter, looking at a phone outside a dealership. Never a hand on the steering wheel staring into the sunset.

Rule 03

Numbers are the artwork.

The most beautiful asset in the product is the price. Imagery exists to give it breathing room — quiet backgrounds, lots of negative space, never competing for attention.
