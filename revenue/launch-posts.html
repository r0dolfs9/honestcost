Phase 1 · Days 1–10 · all copy in Latvian

Launch posts.

Six posts, six surfaces, ten days. Each one ships with a target audience, a copy block ready for paste, and a coach's note on what to keep and what to swap when you adapt it.

The five rules these posts follow

  • Lead with the insight, not the product. "BMW X1 is €2,300/year more than 118i" earns clicks. "Try my calculator" doesn't.
  • One number per post. A single specific figure is more memorable than three.
  • Latvian context wins. "Rīgas vadītājam" beats "the average driver". TEN nodoklis 2025, ne 2023.
  • Self-aware, not self-promoting. "Uztaisīju, jo nevarēju atrast neko līdzīgu LV tirgum" reads as a real person; "Iepazīstinu jums ar revolucionāru rīku" reads as marketing.
  • Sign every post with the URL on its own line. No tracking parameters in shared posts (looks unclean); UTMs are for the affiliate links inside the result.

Post 06 Reddit · r/latvia + r/cars
Day 10 · Wed 17:00 UTC~160 words · English
Audience: bilingual Latvians + tech-curious internationals · methodology-driven · post on r/latvia first, mirror to r/cars 24h later
"I built a Total Cost of Ownership calculator for the Latvian car market. Open methodology — critique welcome."
tl;dr — built a free TCO calculator for Latvia. Real KASKO/OCTA tables, real WLTP-based TEN tax for 2025, real Möller / Inchcape service intervals. Side-by-side comparison of any two new cars over 1–10 years. Why I built it: every TCO calculator I found was either US (irrelevant), UK (irrelevant), or a Latvian dealer's marketing site (not credible). Nothing modelled the actual 2025 transport tax brackets, kW-based OCTA, or KASKO that decreases with residual value. What's modelled: — Leasing PMT with correct residual discounting — Declining KASKO on yearly residual (not flat % of original price) — RBUF (post-warranty repair buffer) with 10% YoY escalation — Service costs from real dealer price sheets — Tyre wear amortized over realistic km — EV-specific: home vs public charging mix, no TEN, lower service What's NOT modelled (honest): — Accident impact on residual value — Used car comparisons (yet) — Lithuanian / Estonian markets (planned) Live: honestcost.lv Methodology: open in the "Kā mēs aprēķinām" section at the bottom of every result. Roast it. If I'm wrong on a number, I'd rather hear it from you than from a dealer.

Keep

The "what's NOT modelled" section earns more upvotes than the feature list. Reddit rewards self-aware authors.

Swap

Read the top comment thread within 2 hours of posting. Reply substantively to the first 3-5 — engagement signals to Reddit's algorithm to push the post.

Cadence after Day 10

After the launch wave, switch to once-a-week "data drop" posts. Pick the most surprising comparison from the week's analytics ("47% of users who compared Yaris Hybrid lost interest after seeing year-3 KASKO — here's why") and post it as a single insight thread. Slow, steady, builds authority. Skip if you have nothing genuinely new.
