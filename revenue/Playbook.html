The wedge thought

HonestCost is the rare "pro-consumer" tool a dealer wants. Why? Because a customer who's already done a TCO comparison and chose your model is a hot lead — they've talked themselves into the car. Dealers pay for that conversion the way they pay for Google Ads, except cleaner.

Monetization layer 1: ship the affiliate links

Two hours of work to add three affiliate slots inside the results screen. Copy-paste from integration-snippets.html. Even at low traffic, this proves whether the "results screen" surface converts at all.

  • OCTA — after the auto-OCTA estimate, "Salīdzini reālās OCTA cenas → octa24.lv" with UTM ?utm_source=honestcost&utm_medium=results&utm_campaign=octa_lookup
  • KASKO — under the KASKO row, "Saņem KASKO piedāvājumu → BALTA · ERGO · BTA" with UTM ?utm_source=honestcost&utm_medium=results&utm_campaign=kasko_3quote
  • Configurator — next to each car name, "Konfigurē pie dīlera →" to the model's official LV configurator (bmw.lv, vw.lv, toyota.lv). Build a small map CONFIGURATOR_URL[carId] in CAR_DB.

Trust constraint

Affiliate links live only inside the result screen, never above the fold, never in the input form. The moment a user feels nudged before they trust the numbers, the tool is dead. Treat this rule as a religion.

Email capture — the optional, low-key one

One small block at the bottom of the result screen: "Saglabā šo salīdzinājumu — atsūtām pa e-pastu kopā ar saiti." EmailJS free tier (200/month) handles delivery; the form posts to a Google Sheet via a Formspree-style endpoint for your own CRM. Code in integration-snippets.html.

Trust section — the "Kā mēs aprēķinām" expandable

Below the results, an expandable section that lists each calculation, each data source, each assumption. This is the single biggest determinant of whether a dealer will trust the tool enough to embed it. Spend the 4 hours.

The pitch — five slides max

The pitch deck (dealer-pitch.html) is five slides only and ends on price. Never present a tool — present a customer in their lobby holding the result.

  1. The problem. "Your customer makes a €40,000 decision based on monthly payment and fuel. They don't know what KASKO costs in year 2."
  2. The mirror. Screenshot of HonestCost showing their car winning vs a competitor — populated live before the meeting.
  3. The hook. Their cars become the default examples on the homepage. Their models seed search. "Saņemt piedāvājumu" button on each of their cars → their CRM.
  4. The proof. Last 30 days HonestCost numbers: X users, Y comparisons, Z minutes average time on result. These are people actively shopping for a car.
  5. The price. €200/month, 3-month minimum, first month free, cancel anytime. We set it up.

The sequence

  1. Build target list of 12. Names, roles, LinkedIn URL, dealer email. 1 hour. W3 · Mon
  2. Personalize the pitch deck for each. Slide 2 mockup uses their car winning vs a real competitor. 10 min × 12. W3 · Tue
  3. Send 12 LinkedIn DMs + 12 follow-up emails 48h later. Templates in dealer-outreach.html. W3 · Wed → W4 · Mon
  4. Book ≥ 4 demo calls. 30 minutes each. Walk them through their own car on the live site. Don't show slides; show the product. W4
  5. Close 1. Offer first month free, no risk. Get a signed PDF (one-page agreement in pricing.html). Even a verbal yes followed by an invoice works. W5–W6
  6. Ship the embed. The "embed" at this stage is literally: their models become the 3 example presets on the homepage. CAR_DB pre-loads their inventory. CTA buttons point to their leadgen form. 4 hours of work. W6

Three things, in parallel

A · Sales — turn the first close into three

  • Make the case study. A one-pager: dealer name, brand, before/after screenshot, "X leads in 30 days at €Y cost-per-lead vs €Z on Google Ads". Their logo is the headline.
  • Re-pitch the original 12 with the case study attached. Same audience, new proof. Expect 30% reply rate where before you got 10%.
  • Pitch the brand HQ, not just dealers. With one BMW-equivalent dealer as proof, approach the importer/distributor (BMW Group Baltic, Möller HQ). They have bigger budgets and can mandate adoption.

B · Product — the embed gets real

The "embed" was a homepage tweak in Phase 2. In Phase 3 it becomes a real white-labeled iframe with three knobs the dealer controls themselves:

  • ?dealer=greenmotors&default_a=skoda_octavia&default_b=vw_golf URL parameters.
  • Dealer logo in the corner of the result card (small, tasteful).
  • Custom CTA URL routed to their CRM with full UTM & comparison-state payload.

Still no backend. Still single-file. Just URL state + a thin DEALER_CONFIGS object in the script.

C · Distribution — the second loop

The first loop (Phase 1 launch) was a push. The second loop is pull:

  • The "shareable result" card. A 1080×1080 SVG → PNG export of the comparison verdict, designed for Instagram / WhatsApp share. "Es ietaupīju €15 067 izvēloties 118i. Tu?" Every share is a free ad.
  • SEO long-tail. Auto-generated comparison pages: /vw-golf-vs-skoda-octavia, /tesla-model-3-vs-bmw-320d. 60 cars × 60 cars × Latvian SEO = a long tail of cheap traffic. One static-site generator pass.
  • Press loop 2. With three months of data, you can sell a story to LA.lv or Delfi: "The 10 cars Latvians compare most — and which one usually wins." Data journalism is catnip for Latvian auto desks.

04 / after €500 mrrThe moat, the multipliers, and the exit

A €500 MRR HonestCost is a side project. A €5k MRR HonestCost is a business. Here are the three multipliers that bridge the gap — pick the one that matches the energy you've got left.

Multiplier A — Baltics

Estonia and Lithuania have identical EU regulatory shape, similar car markets, and only minor tax / service-cost differences. The engine recompiles in a week of focused research per country. Same dealers, three markets. 3× the TAM for ~30% the build.

Multiplier B — Insurance / Finance B2B2C

BTA, BALTA, ERGO, and LATVENERGO all run customer portals that lack a TCO tool. License HonestCost to one of them as a white-label module — €1,500–3,000/month per portal. One contract = 5× a dealer. This is where the real money is.

Multiplier C — Data product

After 12 months of usage data, you know exactly which cars Latvians compare against which. That's a saleable dataset — to dealers (competitive intelligence), to importers (model planning), to media (data journalism partnerships). A quarterly "Latvia Car Decision Report" at €500/seat × 30 buyers = €15k/quarter recurring.

The exit picture (for honesty's sake)

A profitable, growing HonestCost at €3–5k MRR is interesting to: ss.lv / 1a.lv (vertical content acquisition), Inchcape Digital (vertical integration of dealer tools), or a pan-European TCO operator looking for Baltics presence. Realistic multiple: 2.5–4× annual revenue. Don't chase the exit. The strongest exit is the one you don't need.

06 / unknownsWhat we don't know yet — and need to before €500 MRR

  1. Will Latvian dealers actually pay for a third-party tool, or only for ads? Test with one paid pilot at €99/mo if needed. Cheaper than guessing.
  2. Are people sharing results, or saving and forgetting? The share-link metric answers this in Phase 1.
  3. Is OCTA / KASKO affiliate revenue real or vapor? Some Latvian insurers don't run affiliate programs. Confirm payouts before counting them.
  4. Does the EV-vs-ICE comparison drive higher engagement? If yes, double down — EV adoption is the marketing wave to ride 2025–2027.
  5. What's the right per-lead price? Start at €25, A/B test €15 / €25 / €40 across three dealers.
  6. Will a brand HQ (importer, not dealer) sign before a dealer does? Worth one parallel pitch — slower but bigger ticket.

Final note

This plan is built to be wrong in places. Of the 14 artifacts shipped here, expect three to need a rewrite by week 4 once real users show up. That's good. The plan's job is to remove every excuse for not starting, not to predict the shape of the answer.
