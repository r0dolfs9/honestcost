The pipeline

Use the avatar for the face and the product footage for credibility. The avatar should never carry the whole video alone; cut away constantly to proof.

Build the face

Record clear consented footage of yourself in natural light: neutral delivery, skeptical reaction, confident explanation, and a short launch-style ending.

OutputReusable avatar with your actual identity and consent flow complete.

Define repeatable formats

Create series that users recognize: surprising cost comparisons, hidden ownership costs, launch teasers, buyer checklists, and comment-driven car battles.

OutputFive recurring video templates you can post without reinventing the idea.

Write like a human

Use short spoken Latvian. Start with a real reaction, reveal the product proof, give one takeaway, and end before it starts sounding like an ad.

Output15-35 second scripts with a skeptical hook and one clear point.

Generate A-roll

Generate only the talking-head parts with your avatar. Keep each A-roll section short so small synthetic stiffness never gets time to become obvious.

OutputAvatar clips that feel like intros, reactions, and transitions.

Cut to proof

Use HonestCost screen recordings as the credibility layer: two cars side by side, monthly result, saved scenario, methodology, and quick feature flashes.

OutputProduct B-roll that makes the video feel discovered, not manufactured.

Caption everything

Burn in Latvian captions with strong line breaks. Most viewers watch muted, and captions make the performance feel intentional and native.

OutputReadable vertical edit with captions, punch-ins, and quick text hits.

Post today

Publish the strongest short version first. Then turn comments into the next comparison, because user requests are the easiest content engine.

OutputOne daily post plus a feedback loop for tomorrow's script.

Keep trust intact

Use your own avatar, do the platform consent step, avoid fake claims, and keep a light AI-assisted disclosure in the profile, caption, or about copy.

OutputA scalable personal brand that does not depend on misleading people.

Human rules

  • Say less than you want. Cut every sentence that sounds like a pitch deck.
  • Start with surprise, not a brand intro.
  • Cut to screen proof before second 8.
  • Keep avatar shots under 5 seconds at a time.
  • Use light disclosure in your profile or captions. Trust compounds.

Production board

The avatar is the presenter; the screen recording is the evidence. You need both to make it feel like UGC rather than synthetic promo.

A-roll

  • Generate avatar hook from your approved digital twin.
  • Pick a Latvian voice style close to how you actually speak.
  • Use skeptical, casual delivery with short pauses.
  • Export clean vertical clips for editing.

B-roll

  • Record HonestCost two-car comparison.
  • Zoom into the monthly total.
  • Flash cost categories and assumptions.
  • Show saved scenario or methodology for trust.

Quality gate

Before posting, run the edit through this gate. If it fails two or more checks, rewrite or shorten it before publishing.

8/8 post-ready checks

Trust guardrails

  • The avatar is yours and consent is complete.
  • Hook lands in the first two seconds.
  • HonestCost UI appears before second eight.
  • Avatar shot never runs too long without B-roll.
  • Captions are readable on a phone.
  • No fake partnerships, legal claims, or verified-data claims.
  • There is one clear takeaway.
  • There is a comment prompt or next action.
