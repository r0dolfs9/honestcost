01

Script

Turn a rough idea into a short video script that works in English and Latvian before you generate anything.
Next: Voice

What this step is

The script is the control layer. If the script is too long, too formal, or auto-translated badly, every later tool will look fake.

  • 1Write one hook, one proof point, and one takeaway. Keep it 15-35 seconds.
  • 2Create both EN and LV versions manually. Do not rely on auto-translation for Latvian final copy.
  • 3Read the Latvian version out loud. Replace formal words with words you would actually say.
  • 4Mark where B-roll cuts happen: avatar for hook, screen/product proof for the middle, avatar for final line.

02

Voice

Generate the audio outside the avatar tool when Latvian quality matters. This is the fix for weird accent drift.
Next: Avatar

How to complete it

Use the best voice model first, then pass the finished audio into the avatar/lip-sync tool. Voice quality comes before face animation.

  • 1Clone your voice with clean samples, including Latvian if you want Latvian output.
  • 2Generate the same 15-second test in English and Latvian.
  • 3Listen for accent, rhythm, stress, and hard Latvian words like “salīdzinājums” and “apdrošināšana”.
  • 4Export final audio as a clean file. Do not use avatar-tool translation if it makes the accent worse.

03

Avatar

Pick the face layer: your own avatar for trust, or a generic actor for ad testing. Feed it finished audio.
Next: Edit/Post

How to complete it

The avatar should not carry the whole video. It should deliver short human beats while B-roll does the proof.

  • 1Create a consented avatar from your own clean footage, or choose a generic actor for non-personal UGC ads.
  • 2Upload the finished audio from the voice step and generate lip-sync in 9:16.
  • 3Keep avatar clips short: 3-5 seconds before cutting to product or scene footage.
  • 4Export separate A-roll clips so editing stays flexible.

04

Edit/Post

Make it feel real: add B-roll, captions, jump cuts, product proof, and a simple posting loop.
Back to top

How to complete it

This is where synthetic output becomes usable content. B-roll hides avatar stiffness and captions make it native to TikTok/Reels.

  • 1Drop avatar A-roll into the timeline, then cut away to proof every few seconds.
  • 2Add product screen recordings, generated scenes, app footage, or lifestyle B-roll.
  • 3Burn in captions manually for English and Latvian. Check line breaks on phone size.
  • 4Export 9:16, post, then turn comments into the next script.
